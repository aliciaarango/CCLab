[LUMIFLORA CAVAE]

Scientific Name:

[ LUMIFLORA CAVAE ]

Discovered by / at:

Dr. Elysia Thorne, in the year 3000 A.D while on a research expedition in the Waitomo Caves.

Family:

Cactaceae x Arachnocampa luminosa

Habitat:

Waitomo Caves

Appearance:

The flower possesses full rings of petals that softly glow in the dark with shifting color hues due to a mutation with the DNA of Waitomo glowworms. As an evolutionary adaptation, the petals gently rotate and oscillate to purify its low oxygen environment from the pervasive pollutants that have destroyed all other flora. Within the flower’s pollen, a soft off-white color, resides the essence of the flower’s life energy.

Key Characteristics:

  • Glows in the dark.
  • Oscillates to purify oxygen.
  • Life-energy derived from pollen.
  • Human touch lethal.
